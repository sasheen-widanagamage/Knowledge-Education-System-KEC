Chemistry Examination

Welcome to the Chemistry Examination. Please read the following guidelines carefully before starting your exam:

  • Ensure you are in a quiet and comfortable environment free from distractions.
  • You have a total of 120 minutes to complete this exam.
  • Each question carries equal marks. Read each question carefully and provide clear, concise answers.
  • Use appropriate technical terminology where applicable.
  • Click the "Submit" button once you have completed the exam.
  • If you encounter any technical issues, please contact your instructor immediately.

Exam Questions

1. Define the term 'mole' and explain its significance in chemistry. How is it related to Avogadro's number?

2. Describe the difference between ionic and covalent bonds. Provide examples of compounds that illustrate each type of bond.

3. Explain the concept of pH. What does it indicate about a solution, and how is it measured?

4. Discuss the laws of thermodynamics. How do they apply to chemical reactions and energy changes?

5. What is the principle behind chromatography? Explain its applications in separating chemical mixtures.

6. Define and explain the concept of chemical equilibrium. How does Le Chatelier's principle apply to changes in concentration, temperature, and pressure?

7. Define a database and explain the difference between relational and non-relational databases.

8. Describe the structure of an atom. Include details about protons, neutrons, electrons, and isotopes.

9. Explain the difference between endothermic and exothermic reactions. Provide examples of each and describe their energy changes.

10. Discuss the role of catalysts in chemical reactions. How do they affect reaction rates and equilibrium? Provide examples of catalytic processes.
